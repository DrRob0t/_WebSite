## DESIGN IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR DEVELOPERS:

### Section 1 Layout:
- **Hero area:** Full-width with gradient background (suggest dark navy to light blue - aerospace feel)
- **Headline:** 48-60pt bold, white text
- **Three benefits:** Card layout, icons top-left, stat in large text, explanation below
- **Trust signals:** Logos in grayscale, quote below with attribution
- **Stats grid:** 4 columns on desktop, stack on mobile
- **CTA buttons:** Primary (Calculate ROI / Book Demo) in high-contrast color, secondary (See How It Works) as outline

### Section 2 Layout:
- **Split layout:** 50/50 on desktop, stack on mobile (image on top)
- **Before/After comparison:** Side-by-side cards with visual emphasis on density difference
- **Application grid:** 3 columns, equal height cards with icon, title, 3 bullet points, one-liner impact
- **Comparison table:** Sticky header, color-coded checkmarks (green) / warnings (yellow) / crosses (red)
- **Technical credibility:** 3 columns (Patents | Certifications | Research), compact text

## CRITICAL: WHAT NOT TO DO

**Don't:**
- Lead with "About Us" or company history
- Use generic stock photos of engineers or wind tunnels
- Bury the cost savings in paragraph text
- Make the CTA "Contact Us" or "Learn More" (too vague)
- Show sensor technology without showing the DATA it produces
- Claim "aerospace-grade" without showing certifications
- Have more than 2 CTAs per section (decision paralysis)

**Do:**
- Lead with customer outcomes (savings, time, data density)
- Use actual product photos and real data visualizations
- Make savings the hero stat with prominent placement
- Segment CTAs by audience (F1 teams / Aerospace OEMs / Researchers)
- Show pressure maps, not just sensor arrays
- Display certification logos/numbers prominently
- Have primary CTA (high contrast) and secondary (outline) only
